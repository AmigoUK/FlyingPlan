Getting Started

overview introduction navigation roles

FlyingPlan is a web-based drone flight planning and operations management platform. It supports the full lifecycle from customer enquiry through mission planning, pilot assignment, flight execution, and deliverable handoff.

Key areas:

  • Public Customer Form — a 5-step wizard for clients to submit drone survey requests.
  • Admin Dashboard — manage flight plans, assign pilots, plan routes, and export missions.
  • Pilot Portal — accept orders, complete pre-flight risk assessments, upload deliverables.

Use the navigation bar at the top to move between sections. Admins and managers see the full menu; pilots see a streamlined view.

Logging In

login password username session

Navigate to the login page and enter your username and password. After logging in you will be redirected to your role-specific dashboard.

  • Admin / Manager users land on the Admin Dashboard.
  • Pilot users land on the Pilot Dashboard.

Sessions persist until you log out or close your browser. Click Logout in the top-right corner to end your session.

User Roles & Permissions

roles admin manager pilot permissions

FlyingPlan has three user roles with increasing levels of access:

  • Pilot — can view assigned orders, accept/decline work, complete risk assessments, manage their profile, and upload deliverables.
  • Manager — everything a pilot can do, plus access to the admin dashboard, pilot management, and order assignment.
  • Admin — full access, including application settings, branding, job type configuration, and user management.

Customer Request Form

form wizard request enquiry 5-step

The public customer request form is a 5-step wizard that guides clients through submitting a drone survey enquiry. No login is required — the form is publicly accessible.

Each step validates before allowing progression. A progress indicator at the top shows the current step. On submission, a unique reference number is generated and displayed.

Step 1: Customer Details

name email phone company business ABN

Collect the customer's contact information. Fields include:

  • Full name (required)
  • Email (required)
  • Phone number (optional)
  • Customer type — Private or Business
  • Company name & ABN (shown for business customers)
  • Billing contact, email, and purchase order (business only)

Step 2: Job Brief

job type description urgency dates survey

Define the scope of the requested work:

  • Job type — select from the configured list (e.g., Mapping, Inspection, Photography).
  • Description — free-text field for detailed requirements.
  • Urgency — Low, Normal, High, or Urgent.
  • Preferred dates — when the customer would like the work done.
  • Purpose & how they heard about you — from admin-configured dropdown lists.

Step 3: Location & Map

map address pin polygon POI area

Customers specify the job location using an interactive map:

  • Address field — type an address and select from suggestions to centre the map.
  • Pin placement — click on the map to drop a pin at the exact location.
  • Area of interest — optionally draw a polygon to outline the survey boundary.
  • Points of interest — mark specific spots that need attention.

Step 4: Flight Preferences

altitude camera resolution photo mode

Optional technical preferences the customer can specify:

  • Output format — Photos, Video, or Both.
  • Video duration preference (if video selected).
  • Shot types — e.g., nadir, oblique, orbit.
  • Delivery timeline — Standard, Express, or Rush.

Step 5: Review & Submit

review upload consent reference number

The final step shows a summary of all entered information. The customer can:

  • Review all details across all steps.
  • Upload supporting files (site plans, photos, documents).
  • Accept terms and consent.
  • Submit the request — a unique reference number is generated immediately.

Admin Dashboard

dashboard flight plans list overview

The admin dashboard displays all flight plan requests in a sortable, filterable table. Each row shows:

  • Reference number, customer name, job type, and urgency.
  • Status badge (colour-coded).
  • Submission date (with relative date label).

Click any row to open the flight plan detail page for route planning, analysis, and export.

Filters & Search

filter search status job type query

Use the filter controls at the top of the dashboard to narrow results:

  • Status filter — show only plans with a specific status.
  • Job type filter — filter by the type of work requested.
  • Text search — search across reference numbers, customer names, and descriptions.

Flight Plan Statuses

status new in review route planned completed

Flight plans progress through these statuses:

  • New — freshly submitted, not yet reviewed.
  • In Review — being assessed by an admin or manager.
  • Route Planned — waypoints and mission configured.
  • Completed — mission finished.
  • Cancelled — request was cancelled.

Flight Plan Detail Page

detail editor mission planning

The flight plan detail page is the central workspace for mission planning. It combines:

  • Customer request summary (contact, job brief, location).
  • Interactive map with route editor and analysis tools.
  • Waypoint management panel.
  • Drone model selection and GSD calculator.
  • Export buttons for multiple formats.

All changes to waypoints and settings are saved automatically via AJAX — no manual save button needed.

Flight Route Editor

map waypoints Leaflet satellite click drag

The route editor uses a Leaflet-based interactive map. To create a flight route:

  • Click on the map to add waypoints sequentially.
  • Drag waypoint markers to reposition them.
  • Toggle map layers between street view and satellite imagery.
  • Waypoints are connected by lines showing the flight path.

The waypoint panel on the side shows coordinates, altitude, and sequence for each point.

Waypoint Management

save altitude speed heading gimbal action

Each waypoint has configurable parameters:

  • Altitude (metres AGL) — height above ground level.
  • Speed (m/s) — flight speed to this waypoint.
  • Heading (degrees) — aircraft orientation.
  • Gimbal pitch (degrees) — camera angle (-90° = nadir, 0° = horizon).
  • Actions — take photo, start/stop recording, hover.

Waypoints auto-save when modified. Use the delete button to remove individual waypoints, or clear all to start fresh.

Drone Model Selection

DJI Mini 4 Pro Mavic 3 Air 3 sensor

Select the drone model from the dropdown on the flight plan detail page. The selected model determines:

  • Sensor dimensions and focal length (used for GSD calculations).
  • Maximum flight altitude, speed, and duration.
  • Export format compatibility (e.g., Litchi, DJI waylines).

The system supports DJI Mini 4 Pro, Air 3, Mavic 3, Mavic 3 Enterprise, and more. See the Supported Drones reference section for full specs.

Grid Mission Generator

grid parallel crosshatch survey mapping

Generate automated grid survey patterns over a defined area:

  • Draw a polygon on the map to define the survey boundary.
  • Set altitude, overlap (front & side), and angle.
  • Choose parallel (single direction) or crosshatch (double perpendicular).
  • The generator calculates optimal line spacing based on the selected drone's sensor and desired GSD.

Generated waypoints replace the current route. Use coverage analysis afterwards to verify overlap quality.

3D Mapping Grid

oblique double grid multi-angle photogrammetry

For 3D photogrammetry, the oblique/double-grid generator creates multi-angle coverage:

  • Combines a nadir (top-down) grid with oblique passes at configurable camera angles.
  • Supports double-grid patterns (0° and 90°) for comprehensive side coverage.
  • Set gimbal pitch for oblique passes (typically 45°–60°).
  • Ideal for building reconstruction, terrain modelling, and volumetric surveys.

Facade Scanner

facade building inspection standoff vertical

Generate vertical scan patterns for building inspections:

  • Define the facade line by clicking two points on the map (base of the wall).
  • Set standoff distance — how far the drone flies from the facade.
  • Set min/max altitude for the vertical scan range.
  • Configure overlap between scan rows.
  • The generator creates a serpentine pattern flying up and down along the facade.

Mission Patterns

orbit spiral cable cam multi-altitude orbit

Pre-built mission patterns for common scenarios:

  • Orbit — circular path around a point of interest at a set radius and altitude.
  • Multi-altitude orbit — stacked orbits at different heights for full vertical coverage.
  • Spiral — ascending/descending spiral for tower or chimney inspections.
  • Cable cam — straight-line flight between two points at constant altitude.

All patterns generate waypoints that can be edited individually after creation.

GSD Calculator

GSD resolution cm/pixel overlap coverage

The Ground Sample Distance calculator shows the spatial resolution of imagery based on:

  • Drone model (sensor size and focal length).
  • Flight altitude (AGL).

Outputs include GSD in cm/pixel, single image footprint dimensions, and recommended photo interval for the desired overlap percentage. Lower altitude = finer detail but smaller coverage per image.

Coverage Analysis

overlap heatmap gaps photo coverage

After generating a grid or route, run coverage analysis to verify photo overlap:

  • A colour-coded heatmap overlay shows overlap density across the survey area.
  • Green = sufficient overlap (≥60%), Yellow = marginal, Red = insufficient.
  • Gap detection highlights areas with zero coverage.
  • Statistics panel shows min/max/average overlap percentages.

Aim for at least 70% front overlap and 60% side overlap for reliable photogrammetry.

Quality Report

photogrammetry 3D reconstruction convergence

The quality report predicts photogrammetry reconstruction quality based on the planned mission:

  • Convergence angle analysis between overlapping images.
  • Expected tie-point density estimation.
  • 3D reconstruction confidence score.
  • Recommendations for improving mission parameters if quality is predicted to be low.

3D Mission Preview

Three.js terrain mesh visualisation

Visualise the planned mission in 3D using the Three.js-powered preview:

  • Terrain mesh rendered from elevation data.
  • Flight path shown as a 3D line with waypoint markers.
  • Camera frustums indicating photo footprints on the ground.
  • Orbit controls — drag to rotate, scroll to zoom, right-click to pan.

Elevation & Terrain Following

elevation AGL terrain follow chart

The elevation profile shows ground height along the flight path:

  • Chart displays terrain elevation (AMSL) and planned flight altitude.
  • AGL (Above Ground Level) is calculated at each waypoint.
  • Terrain following mode adjusts waypoint altitudes to maintain constant AGL over varying terrain.
  • Elevation data is fetched from the Open Elevation API.

Weather & Airspace

weather wind FRZ airspace restrictions

Check weather conditions and airspace restrictions for the flight location:

  • Weather — current temperature, wind speed/direction, visibility, cloud cover, and precipitation.
  • Airspace — shows nearby Flight Restriction Zones (FRZs), controlled airspace, and NOTAMs.
  • Data is fetched for the centroid of the flight plan's location.

Always verify conditions against your operations manual before flying.

Export Formats

KMZ KML GeoJSON CSV GPX Litchi DJI

Export your flight plan in multiple formats:

  • KMZ/KML — Google Earth compatible, includes waypoints and flight path.
  • GeoJSON — standard geospatial format for GIS software.
  • Enhanced GeoJSON — includes altitude, speed, heading, and gimbal data per waypoint.
  • CSV — spreadsheet-compatible waypoint list.
  • GPX — GPS exchange format for navigation apps.
  • Litchi CSV — compatible with the Litchi flight app for DJI drones.

Importing KMZ Files

import upload DJI waylines

Import existing flight plans from KMZ files:

  • Click the Import KMZ button on the flight plan detail page.
  • Select a .kmz file from your device.
  • The system extracts waypoints (coordinates, altitude) and loads them into the route editor.
  • Supports standard KMZ and DJI wayline formats.

Imported waypoints replace the current route. Export first if you want to keep the existing plan.

Path Tools

reverse straighten offset route editing

Quick-edit tools for modifying the flight path:

  • Reverse — flip the order of all waypoints (fly the route backwards).
  • Straighten — remove intermediate waypoints, keeping only start and end.
  • Offset — shift the entire route by a specified distance and bearing.

Sharing & Duplicating

share link duplicate token read-only

Share flight plans with external stakeholders:

  • Share link — generates a unique token-based URL that shows the mission map and waypoints in read-only mode. No login required.
  • Duplicate — create a copy of the flight plan for variation planning or template reuse.

Shared links can be revoked at any time by regenerating the token.

Orders Overview

orders assign pilot management

The Orders section (available to managers and admins) tracks the operational side of flight work:

  • View all orders with status, assigned pilot, and linked flight plan.
  • Filter by status, pilot, or date range.
  • Each order is linked to a specific flight plan request.

Creating & Assigning Orders

create assign pilot schedule

To create an order from a flight plan:

  • Open the flight plan detail page.
  • Click Create Order and select a pilot from the available list.
  • Set the scheduled date and any special instructions.
  • The pilot receives the assignment on their dashboard.

Pilot availability status (Available / On Mission / Unavailable) helps you choose the right person.

Order Status Workflow

assigned accepted in progress delivered

Orders follow this lifecycle:

  • Pending Assignment — order created, no pilot yet.
  • Assigned — pilot selected, awaiting acceptance.
  • Accepted — pilot confirmed the job.
  • In Progress — pilot is actively working (flight or post-processing).
  • Flight Complete — flying done, awaiting deliverables.
  • Delivered — files uploaded and handed off.
  • Closed — order finalised.
  • Declined — pilot declined the assignment.

Deliverables

upload files photos videos download

After completing a flight, pilots upload deliverables through the order detail page:

  • Drag-and-drop or click to upload photos, videos, and processed files.
  • Add descriptions to each deliverable.
  • Admins can view and download all uploaded files.
  • Marking deliverables as complete advances the order to "Delivered" status.

Pilot Dashboard

pilot orders assigned active

The pilot dashboard shows all orders assigned to you:

  • Active orders — jobs you've accepted that need action.
  • Pending orders — new assignments waiting for your acceptance.
  • Completed orders — past jobs for reference.

Click any order to view its full details, flight plan, and customer requirements.

Pilot Order Detail

accept decline notes export

From the order detail page, pilots can:

  • Accept or Decline the assignment.
  • View the full flight plan with map, waypoints, and route.
  • Export the mission in various formats for your flight controller.
  • Add notes visible to the admin team.
  • Update order status as work progresses.
  • Upload deliverables when the job is complete.

Pre-Flight Risk Assessment

safety checklist IMSAFE pre-flight

Before each flight, complete the mandatory risk assessment:

  • IMSAFE checklist — Illness, Medication, Stress, Alcohol, Fatigue, Emotion (pilot fitness).
  • Site assessment — obstacles, people, animals, electromagnetic interference.
  • Weather check — wind, visibility, precipitation conditions.
  • Airspace check — FRZ proximity, NOTAMs, controlled airspace.
  • Equipment check — battery levels, propeller condition, firmware updates.

The assessment must be completed before the order can progress to "In Progress".

Pilot Profile

qualifications insurance Flying ID GVC

Manage your professional profile from the Profile page:

  • Personal details — name, email, phone, address.
  • Flyer ID & Operator ID — CAA registration numbers and expiry dates.
  • Qualifications — A2 CofC, GVC (Multi-Rotor), GVC (Fixed-Wing), practical competency.
  • Insurance — provider, policy number, and expiry date.
  • Bio — brief description of your experience and specialisations.

Certifications & Equipment

certifications equipment documents serial

Track your certifications and equipment inventory:

  • Certifications — add qualification records with document uploads and expiry dates.
  • Memberships — professional body memberships (e.g., ARPAS-UK).
  • Equipment — register drones, batteries, and accessories with serial numbers and purchase dates.
  • Documents — upload and manage operations manuals, insurance certificates, and other documentation.

Expiry date warnings appear when certifications are approaching renewal.

Settings (Admin Only)

branding business name logo job types form

Admins can customise the application from the Settings page:

  • Business name — displayed in the browser title and public form.
  • Primary colour — theme accent colour used throughout the interface.
  • Job types — add, edit, reorder, or deactivate the list of job types available on the customer form.
  • Purpose options — configure the "Purpose of footage" dropdown.
  • Heard about us — configure the "How did you hear about us?" dropdown.

Tips & Shortcuts

tips productivity map controls best practices
  • Double-click the map to add a waypoint quickly.
  • Scroll wheel zooms the map; hold Shift + drag to zoom to a rectangle.
  • Use satellite view when planning over rural or vegetated areas for better context.
  • Export to Litchi CSV for the most feature-rich third-party flight execution.
  • Run coverage analysis before exporting to catch overlap gaps early.
  • Use terrain following for hilly sites to maintain consistent GSD.
  • Share read-only links with clients for pre-flight approval — no account needed.
  • Check weather & airspace on the day of the flight, not just during planning.

Glossary

GSD AGL FRZ NOTAM KMZ nadir oblique gimbal
AGL (Above Ground Level)
Altitude measured from the terrain surface directly below the drone.
AMSL (Above Mean Sea Level)
Altitude measured from mean sea level — used for airspace and chart references.
CofC (Certificate of Competency)
CAA qualification (e.g., A2 CofC) allowing specific drone operations.
FRZ (Flight Restriction Zone)
Airspace around airports and other sensitive locations where drone flight is restricted or prohibited.
GSD (Ground Sample Distance)
The real-world size of one pixel in a drone photo, measured in cm/pixel. Lower = higher detail.
GVC (General VLOS Certificate)
UK CAA qualification for commercial drone operations within Visual Line of Sight.
Gimbal
The stabilised mount holding the drone's camera. Pitch angle controls whether the camera points down (nadir) or forward (oblique).
GPX (GPS Exchange Format)
XML-based format for GPS tracks and waypoints, compatible with most mapping and navigation software.
KML (Keyhole Markup Language)
XML format for geographic data, viewable in Google Earth and GIS applications.
KMZ
Compressed (zipped) KML file. Standard format for importing/exporting flight plans.
Nadir
Camera pointing straight down (gimbal pitch -90°). Used for mapping and orthophoto generation.
NOTAM (Notice to Airmen)
Official notice of temporary airspace changes, hazards, or restrictions.
Oblique
Camera angled between horizontal and nadir (typically 30°–60° from vertical). Used for facade capture and 3D modelling.
Overlap (Front / Side)
Percentage of shared area between consecutive photos. Higher overlap improves photogrammetry quality but increases flight time.
Photogrammetry
Process of creating 3D models, maps, or measurements from overlapping photographs.
